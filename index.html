THE BRAIN RUNNER GAME

WHO IS IT FOR

Rate of people having Alzheimer’s disease is increasing everyday and it is becoming difficult for older people to memorize things properly. In order to check the level of their illness, we have made a game which can detect whether a person is affected or not. Along with this, game when played regularly, would help in sharpening the memory as well.

OVERVIEW

Our game works on an algorithm which forces the brain to think and memorize it more effectively. A maze is shown with the correct path highlighted in the beginning which is to be remembered. Simple and elegant design, challenge the maze for various missions.

THE POSITIVES

  • This is game is highly motivating and intresting.
  • Great exercise for the brain.
  • User can continue to play even if he/she hits the wall and still finish it.
  • Helps those patients who are at risk of developing Alzheimer disease.

SIGNS AND SYMPTOMPS

Early stage Alzheimer's:
  • Not remembering episodes of forgetfulness
  • Forgets names of family or friends
Middle stage Alzheimer's:
  • Greater difficulty remembering recently learned information
  • Deepening confusion in many circumstances
last stage Alzheimer's:
  • Poor ability to think
  • More abusive, anxious, or paranoid
PREVENTIVE MEASURES

Manage your numbers: Do you know if your blood pressure, blood sugar, and cholesterol are too high? Research shows strong connections between Alzheimer’s and conditions like high blood pressure, high cholesterol, type 2 diabetes, and heart disease

Check your weight: If you have a lot of weight to lose, and start to work on shedding those pounds and keeping them off, if also could help lower your risk. One study found that obesity can change the brain in a way that raises your odds of getting Alzheimer’s.

Exercise your body:When you work out, even a little bit, more blood flows to the brain, which makes your brain healthier. Aim for at least 30 minutes of exercise, 5 or more days per week.
